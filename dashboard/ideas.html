Proposals from today's review of the system. Each one is a specific, buildable idea — not a rewrite. Mockups below are rendered with real typography and motion, not screenshots.

April 17 2026 · 8 proposals · Reviewed against the mission

01
A Story Strip on the Live tab
Medium

"49 species" is a scorecard. A sentence is a story.

The API already emits the raw material for stories — first-sighting flags, hourly visit consensus, BirdNET audio-only detections, trust levels. The dashboard reduces it to counts and grids. This strip lives above Recently Seen; cards rotate every 20 seconds, each generated from existing endpoints. Taps link into the rigorous view.

Every sentence above is a direct template fill against fields already in classifications.db + birdnet_local.db + the visits table. No new data. Just editorial decisions.

03
Honest typography for what the system isn't sure about
Small

The model is lying, and right now the UI amplifies the lie.

Every sighting currently reads "Northern Cardinal 100% ✓" — even when the same bird got called three different species across three frames. We already compute trust_level and prior_reason. This treats different confidence tiers with different typographic weight. Italic for likely, grey dim for sorting it out, full weight only for truly-locked. Hover reveals the reason: "Time implausible: American Goldfinch seen 7:00–15:00."

Before · Every sighting looks certain
Northern Cardinal 100% ✓
Song Sparrow 100% ✓
House Finch 100% ✓
Black-capped Chickadee 100% ✓

All four are the same bird, seconds apart. The UI doesn't say so.

After · The system shows its thinking

American Goldfinch Time implausible — goldfinches seen 7–15h, not 6pm
small brown bird 2 of 3 frames disagreed — waiting for vote lock
Tufted Titmouse 5 frames · audio corroborated 12s earlier

Smallest fix in this document. Pure display logic over fields the API already returns. Biggest trust payoff.

Casual and obsessive converge here — kid gets eye candy, birder gets co-occurrence data.

07
Give silence a voice
Small

An observatory on a kitchen wall is observed in glances. It must be interesting in the silences, or it becomes wallpaper nobody looks at.

When no detections for >5 minutes, the yard panel shifts into ambient mode. Current hour's typical activity based on historical data, plus one trivia line from the species-info library for a recently-seen bird. The dashboard starts having an editorial voice, not just a pulse.

The yard is quiet
Usually 8 visits this hour in mid-April. Today: 2 so far. The last visitor was a Tufted Titmouse, 14 minutes ago.
Did you know Tufted Titmice hoard seeds one at a time, flying back and forth from feeder to a cache tree — often the same tree for years.

"8 visits this hour in mid-April" is a one-line SQL aggregation precomputed nightly. Trivia comes from species_info.json which we already have (230 species covered).

08
Classify as a field journal, not a triage queue
Medium

The fastest path to deadly accurate is a reviewer who enjoys reviewing.

Right now each review card is a frame + buttons. The context — what the track looked like across its five detection frames, what else was audible that minute — exists in the data but isn't on the card. This makes the review a moment: frame at center, a filmstrip above showing the bird's other frames from the same visit, and one sentence below giving temporal context. The reviewer is editing a field-journal entry, not triaging a queue.

6:14am, after a Tufted Titmouse visit. Heard in the same minute: Wood Thrush 82%, Song Sparrow 71%.

Wrong species
Not a bird

The filmstrip groups by track_id (already in pipeline.db). The context line is one API call that joins classifications + birdnet_local by timestamp window. Keyboard shortcuts preserved — but the card becomes something you'd want to sit with.

09
The yard has a heartbeat
Small

Every species has an hour it prefers. Together they make a day.

A single horizontal block that answers "when does which bird show up?" in one glance. Each row is a species; each cell is an hour of the day, colored by that species' visit density at that hour over the last 30 days. Sorted by peak hour — early birds float to the top, dusk feeders to the bottom. Dawn and dusk bands softly tinted. This is the simplest possible visualization that turns a query result into a piece of music.

10
The Season Stream
Medium

When birds arrive. When they leave. Who stays all year.

A year as 52 weekly columns, species as rows. Dots where the bird was present that week, bright gold for peak weeks. Year-round residents run as continuous ribbons. Seasonal migrants appear as bands that shrink and grow. Rare visitors show up as lonely specks. This visualization gets more beautiful every year you run the system — multi-year overlays show which winters had the goldfinches and which springs had the warblers. Our 142K row database is already the skeleton.

11
Who flocks with whom
Medium

Every yard has a social network. Ours has 142,000 data points of it.

A graph showing which species tend to visit within 5 minutes of each other. Thicker edges = more co-occurrences. Hubs float to the center. The classic Chickadee–Titmouse–Nuthatch mixed-flock should emerge on its own — and so should surprises. "Juncos and Song Sparrows ride together, but Cardinals come alone." Nodes are clickable, revealing who-with-whom over time. This is ecology you can see, pulled straight out of the timestamps.

12
Heard vs seen
Small

The birds the cameras miss. The birds the mics miss. The birds we know beyond doubt.

The observatory has two sensory systems — cameras and BirdNET audio — and most dashboards would treat one of them as primary. This view makes the gap between them the subject. A Venn diagram of heard-only, seen-only, and both. Heard-only species are usually canopy birds that never land: Wood Thrush, Veery, Great Crested Flycatcher. Seen-only species are often quiet at the feeder: Juncos, Titmice. Both is the confidently-present core. The shape of the Venn tells you what kind of yard you have.

Small

Every year, a new first. And after three years, a ritual.

A ship's log, typographically rendered. Each year gets a column. Each entry is a species and the date it first appeared that year. Species tagged "new this year" when they've never been recorded before — those become heirloom moments. After three or four years, the comparisons start to tell stories: "The goldfinches arrived two weeks earlier this year." "The Chipping Sparrow hasn't come back yet." Small

Sports needs box scores. An observatory needs records.

A grid of small cards — each one a superlative, auto-updated nightly. Biggest day. Quietest morning. The rarest visitor. The bird that flew solo. The most dawn arrivals. 15
The Reviewer's Duel
Medium

Catching the model's mistakes is the game. The more confident it was, the sweeter the catch.

Every bird is a matchup: the model made a guess, you're the ref. Each card shows the model's claim with its (almost always) 100% confidence, flags when the bird was a within-track flipper (four species guessed across five frames = suspicious), and invites you to call it. Correct confirms move the streak. Corrections of overconfident-wrongs get gold. Streaks compound. At 20 reviews you're done for the day — summary card with stats, maybe a badge, back to your life.

The difference from idea 08 isn't the card layout — it's the stance. 08 is a field journal: you're a naturalist entering observations. 15 is a sport: the model is on the field, you're the ref with a whistle. Same data, same review, very different feel. Both are compatible — the Duel could be the default mode with an "editorial mode" toggle for when you want to take your time.

Game mechanics: streaks, accuracy, daily cap, scoring. But scoring is internal motivation, never a public leaderboard that makes someone else's opinion part of the ritual. A daily badge. A streak counter you see only when you log in. Nothing shareable by default. The ambient goal is cleaner training data; the felt experience is playing, which is why you keep coming back.

The same data powers both this and idea 08 (Field Journal). Toggle between "Duel" and "Journal" modes on the Classify tab — or have the mode follow the content (fast for confident-but-wrong frames, slow for ambiguous multi-birds). Keyboard shortcuts from 08 preserved exactly so muscle memory carries over.

Where these came from: Three reviewers were dispatched on April 17 2026 after a long session rebuilding the overlay, AOI, and snapshot pipeline. One audited every doc for truth drift; one looked for performance and data-integrity bugs; one reviewed the experience against the mission of delightful + deadly accurate + beautiful stories.

The critical bugs they found — a 10-line softmax_top3 math error that explains the yard model's 100%-everything behavior, a non-transactional apply_verdict that is almost certainly the "data drift" David suspected, species-directory names that disagree across three consumers — are being tracked separately as fixes. This page is the creative half: the proposals that are less about fixing and more about becoming.

Each mockup above uses real typography (Playfair Display + Outfit + Fraunces), real motion where applicable, and real proportions. None of them are screenshots. Every one is a building block for something the observatory could actually be, this month.
